# How to Isolate Chloroplasts from Spinach　ホウレン草から葉緑体を単離する方法

### By [Hiroo Komine](https://github.com/BioClub/lab/wiki/BHA4-HIROO-%E4%B8%AD%E4%BA%8C%E7%97%85PROJECT), [Sota Kaneko](https://github.com/BioClub/lab/wiki/BHA4-Sota-Kaneko) and [Yumi Nishihara](https://florafaunafab.wordpress.com/)

[Chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast) are *a specialised compartment in plant cells and algae where [photosynthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photosynthesis) takes place; originally derived from photosynthetic bacteria called [cyanobacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria)* [1]

This protocol is based on the instructions by [BioHack Academy](http://biohackacademy.github.io/bha4/class/7/chloroplast-isolation/).

1. Cut a plastic folder and fold a rack for the centrifuge tubes (Inspired by [Ohta-san's work](https://github.com/Drunkar/folding_test_tube_rack)).  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33157033830/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-02"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2873/33157033830_6a4c344bdb.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-02"></a>
2. Wash your hands and don’t forget to tie back long hair!
3. Wash glassware with dish washing liquid and rinse well.
4. Create **100 mL of 0.5 M [sucrose](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/sucrose#section=Top)** in a 100ml flask [2].  
   ```
   End molarity (moles/L) * end volume (L) * molecular weight (g/mole) = necessary grams  
   0.5 moles/L * 0.1L * 342.2965 g/mole = 17.114825 g  
   ```
   Measure **17g** of granulated sugar and pour in demi water until the solution measures **100ml**. Cover the flask with aluminium foil, swill it until the sugar melts, and store it on frozen blueberries.  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33412022001/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-04"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/4/3950/33412022001_16179fa2f3.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-04"></a> <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33412022921/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-05"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2852/33412022921_b5b2db86dc.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-05"></a>  
5. Wash spinach leaves with water. Cut the leaves into small pieces with scissors　and cut out the veins. ~~Measure 10 grams~~
6. At first we tried to grind the leaves in the blender but that didn't work out well.   
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33540673345/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-06"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2922/33540673345_b4db5f04b3.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-06"></a> <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33157032840/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-07"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/4/3952/33157032840_e9863abd95.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-07"></a>  
   So we crushed the leaves in a spice grinder and then measured **10 grams**.  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33540685815/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-08"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2825/33540685815_a4969464a7.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-08"></a> <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33157022460/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-09"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2927/33157022460_47faeeac6e.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-09"></a>  
7. Add **5 ml** of 0.5 M sucrose to the solution and mix.  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33540685385/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-10"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2875/33540685385_5f14bb7489.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-10"></a>
8. Filter the mixture through the coffee filter into a 200ml beaker.  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33157021730/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-11"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2806/33157021730_26fb7d90e4.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-11"></a> <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33157003160/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-12"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2864/33157003160_79898bbf65.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-12"></a>  
9. Save **0.5 ml** of the filtrate in a tube for comparison later on (Tube No.0).
10. Transfer two times **1.5 ml** to two centrifuge tubes. Make sure they’re balanced.  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33157021300/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-13"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/4/3956/33157021300_939e2e5414.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-13"></a>  
11. Centrifuge ~~at low speed (50x - 200x g)~~ for **10 minutes** to get rid of cell debris (white pellet)  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/32726132633/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-14"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2828/32726132633_39510716c6.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-14"></a> <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/32697109364/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-15"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2816/32697109364_cf0c8d8077.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-15"></a>  
12. Transfer the supernatant to two fresh centrifuge tubes. Save 0.5 mL of each tube for later examination (Tube No.1-1 and 2-1).
13. Centrifuge ~~at high speed (1000x g)~~ for **10 minutes** to pellet the chloroplasts.
14. Dispose the supernatant when you feel satisfied with the pellet. (Spin longer if you feel like it).  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33412011291/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-16"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/4/3927/33412011291_daba7ede8b.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-16"></a>
15. Create a solution of 80% acetone in a 50ml beaker. Measure **8ml** of acetone and pour in demi water until the solution measures **10ml**. Cover beaker with aluminium foil.
16. Add **0.5 ml** of 80% acetone to a tube and resuspend the pellet (Tube No.1-2 and 2-2).  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/32697065964/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-17"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/4/3938/32697065964_29258874c6.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-17"></a>  
17. Examine the samples under the microscope (or measure the absorbance at OD650). Count the number of Chloroplasts per mL.  
   <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/33156983320/in/album-72157677327943603/" title="week07-18"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/3/2907/33156983320_cc68ed4995.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-18"></a> <a data-flickr-embed="true"  href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/130922490@N08/32726431403/in/dateposted/" title="week07-20"><img src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/4/3682/32726431403_42cc7cacb8.jpg" width="425" height="319" alt="week07-20"></a>  

Dispose the acetone solution by absorbing it with paper towels and sealing it in a bag.
